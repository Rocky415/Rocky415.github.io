Pneumatic Exoskeleton

This device amplifies my bicep/tricep movements through a pneumatic cylinder located behind my tricep. Here is a video that shows the exoskeleton lifting a 24kg weight:

The exoskeleton is able to lift 24kg of weight on its own. I am trying my best to completely relax my arm in this video, although it is not perfect. I have a journal pending publication at Columbia Junior Science Journal. I will link it here after publication. It details the experimentation, material selection and design of this prototype.

Key Features:

  • +25kg lifting capacity
  • Relatively lightweight, 3D-printed out of polycarbonate
  • EMG sensor expansion planned
